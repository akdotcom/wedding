The story of a computer-science-math-brain falling for the technophobic-social-worker is just your typical love story. Boy meets girl. Boy thinks girl is cute. Girl thinks boy is cute. They are the last ones at the party and ride home on the subway together. He rides 3 extra stops on the train to keep talking with her.

And that's the story of how Sarah and Neil met. Now, after two happy years, she is a little less afraid of the internet and he is a little less disapproving when she counts with her fingers.

The King of Prussia Mall is the largest shopping mall on the east coast, if you're into that sort of thing.

Valley Forge National Park is a very pretty national park about half an hour away from Normandy Farm. It's the same Valley Forge that George Washington stayed with his army all winter during the revolutionary war, so it's interesting to history buffs and nature lovers.

Peddler's Village is a fair/village with a bunch of artisan shops that adults might find interesting, and an area for kids that Sarah's sister Rachel described as "Chuck E Cheese on crack." The kids' area is called Giggleberry Fair. Peddler's village is near the village of New Hope, PA, which has a similar vibe and more stuff like it.
